TASK 5

Now read this text to check your answers.

The computer mouse is a hand-operator device that lets you control more easily the location of the pointer on your screen. You can make selections and choices with the mouse button.
  The mouse contains a rubber-coated ball that resets on the surface of your working area or mousemat. When the mouse is moved over that surface, the ball rolls. The ball’s movements up and down, and left and right, turn the two axles inside the mouse. As they turn, detectors register the changing position. A small integrated circuit inside the mouse sends signals to the operating system, which instructs it to move the pointer on your screen.
