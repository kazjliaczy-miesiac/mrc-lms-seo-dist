READING: CHOOSING A PROFESSION.

TASK 1

Read the text «Choosing a profession ». Make a list of new words and expressions.

Choosing a profession is a major event in every young person's life.

It is time of new freedoms and opportunities, but one has to make one of these choices:

1. Go to a university or to college for further education. They do this not just to be educated but also to increase their chances in the lottery of employment. There are such colleges in most towns. Students are able to study in different ways, including full-time, part-time, evening study.

2. Find a job (which may or may not include some part-time study paid for by the employer).

3. Go on a government training scheme for the young unemployed.

Some will do none of these things but be unemployed. In this case, they have to look for jobs.

Looking for a job is a in itself, so it is important to get yourself self-assessed. Look at yourself realistically, at your experience, your strengths and weaknesses, likes and dislikes and decide. What are you good at, not so good at, what sort of things you want to do and can do. Use all possible sources to help you get careers advice:

- employment agency;

- job center;

- national and local newspapers;

- professional newspapers and journals;

- local radio stations;

- friends and relatives.
